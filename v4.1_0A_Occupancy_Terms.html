v4.1_0A Occupancy Terms

Hover over italicized for brief description.

General Terms

Majority of cycles: Calculated as floor((cycles / 2) + 0.5) + 1 -- e.g., 6 for 10 cycles
CBI: Called Base Intensity (highest intensity of the 4 channels; average CBI refers to average across number of cycles analyzed)
SHI: ratio of Second Highest Intensity to the highest intensity
Chastity: ratio of the highest intensity to the top two intensities
MultiCall: DNBs with at least two intensities above the empty threshold
Mixed: DNBs with multiple calls above background and/or are of low chastity (<0.7) for majority of cycles
Splits: DNBs that belong to a cluster/family of adjacent DNBs that share a common sequence or intensity pattern (across majority of cycles)
SNR: Signal-to-Noise Ratio
Valid: Non-Empty, Non-Outlier DNBs

Seeds+Singular (%ofTotal), smSeeds+smSingular (%ofSmall)
Seeds and singular DNBs. A seed is the DNB with the highest avg CBI of a splits cluster/family. Singular DNBs are not split, mixed, empty or an outlier. Seeds and singular DNBs should be exclusive.
Singular (%ofTotal), smSingular (%ofSmall)
Singular DNBs are not split, mixed, empty or an outlier.
Empty (%ofTotal)
DNBs with a mean average Called Base Intensity (CBI) less than the empty threshold are declared empty. The empty threshold is calculated by measuring the distribution of noise/background in each channel of each cycle. See Empty Threshold.
<0.20 (%ofTotal)
DNBs with an avg CBI less than 0.20.
NE<0.25 (%ofTotal)
Non-Empty DNBs with an avg CBI less than 0.25.
NE<0.50 (%ofTotal)
Non-Empty DNBs with an avg CBI less than 0.50.
Mixed (%ofTotal), vMixed (%ofValid)
Mixed DNBs either have multiple calls above background or are of low chastity (or both). See MultiCall, LowChastity.
MultiCall (%ofTotal), vMultiCall (%ofValid)
MultiCall DNBs have at least two intensities above the empty threshold (in a given cycle) for the majority of the cycles being analyzed.
LowChastity (%ofTotal), vLowChastity (%ofValid)
Chastity is calculated as the ratio of the highest intensity to the top two intensities (in a given cycle). A DNB is declared to have low chastity if it has a chastity lower than 0.7 for the majority of cycles being analyzed.
Spatial Duplicates (%ofTotal)
Splits sequences that are duplicates
Seq Splits (%ofTotal), vSeq Splits (%ofValid)
Non-seed splits that match at least one neighbor by sequence (may be reduntant).
Int Splits (%ofTotal), vInt Splits (%ofValid)
Non-seed splits that are also hidden splits, which are intensity-based matches previously undiscovered by sequence-based matching.
Normalization Factor
The inverse of which is used to multiply the avg CBI distribution so that the 50th percentile becomes 1.0. Thresholds are scaled accordingly as well.
Total # DNBs
Count of DNBs in that particular FOV.
Empty Threshold
Gaussian mixture models are used to characterize the distribution of noise/background in a given channel. An indivual empty threshold is 3 sigma above the mean of the determined distribution. The value reported here is the mean average of all channels/cycles being analyzed.
Small Threshold
A small threshold is calculated as the 25th percentile of intensities above the empty threshold in a given cycle. The value reported here is the mean average of all channels/cycles being analyzed.
Large Threshold
A large threshold is calculated as the 75th percentile of intensities above the empty threshold in a given cycle. The value reported here is the mean average of all channels/cycles being analyzed.
Outlier Threshold
An outlier threshold is calculated as the 75th percentile of intensities above the empty threshold plus 3 times the difference between the 75th percentile and the 25th percentile in a given cycle (Q3 + 3 (Q3-Q1). The value reported here is the mean average of all channels/cycles being analyzed.
RHO Avg/A/C/G/T Intercept
RHO intensities are computed by transforming the normalized intensities (crosstalk, phasing correction) back to raw intensities scale. The RHO intensity values for the cycle range are fitted and the intercept is reported. RHO Avg Intercept is the intercept of the fitted average line of channels A, C, G, and T.
RHO Avg/A/C/G/T C01
The RHO intensity value of the first cycle of the cycle range.
<0.15 (%ofTotal)
DNBs with an avg CBI rank less than 15%.
<0.20 (%ofTotal)
DNBs with an avg CBI rank less than 20%.
<0.25 (%ofTotal)
DNBs with an avg CBI rank less than 25%.
Empty (%ofTotal)
DNBs with an mean average Called Base Intensity (CBI) less than the empty threshold are declared empty. The empty threshold is calculated by measuring the distribution of noise/background in each channel of each cycle. See Empty Threshold.
Small (%ofTotal)
DNBs with an avg CBI greater than or equal to the empty threshold but less than the small threshold are declared small. See Empty Threshold, Small Threshold.
Med (%ofTotal)
DNBs with an avg CBI greater than or equal to the small threshold but less than the large threshold are declared large. See Small Threshold, Large Threshold.
Large (%ofTotal)
DNBs with an avg CBI greater than or equal to the larger threshold but less than the outlier threshold are declared large. See Large Threshold, Outlier Threshold.
Outlier (%ofTotal)
DNBs with an avg CBI greater than or equal to the outlier threshold are declared outliers. See Outlier Threshold.
Avg SNR
Average signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) for all 4 channels. SNR is calculated as the trimmed (2%) mean of called (maximum) intensities (in a given channel) divided by the standard deviation of the non-called (not maximum) intensities.
SNR A
Average signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) for A channel.
SNR C
Average signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) for C channel.
SNR G
Average signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) for G channel.
SNR T
Average signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) for T channel.
0-Call (%ofTotal), v0-Call (%ofValid)
0-Call DNBs have 0 intensities above the empty threshold (in a given cycle) for the majority of the cycles being analyzed.
1-Call (%ofTotal), v1-Call (%ofValid)
1-Call DNBs have 1 intensities above the empty threshold (in a given cycle) for the majority of the cycles being analyzed.
2-Call (%ofTotal), v2-Call (%ofValid)
2-Call DNBs have 2 intensities above the empty threshold (in a given cycle) for the majority of the cycles being analyzed.
3-Call (%ofTotal), v3-Call (%ofValid)
3-Call DNBs have 3 intensities above the empty threshold (in a given cycle) for the majority of the cycles being analyzed.
4-Call (%ofTotal), v4-Call (%ofValid)
4-Call DNBs have all 4 intensities above the empty threshold (in a given cycle) for the majority of the cycles being analyzed.
[n n+10)% SHI (%ofTotal), [n n+10)% vSHI (%ofValid)
DNBs with average Second Highest Intensity proportion of highest intensity in the range of [n, n + 10)% for the majority of cycles being analyzed. The ranges include decile groups from [0, 10)% to [90, 100)%.
100% SHI (%ofTotal), 100% vSHI (%ofValid)
DNBs with 100% SHI for the majority of cycles being analyzed. This would occur if the top two intensities are equal -- i.e., there is no highest intensity.
NaN-Chastity (%ofTotal), vNaN-Chastity (%ofValid)
DNBs with chastity values with NaN for the majority of cycles being analyzed.
0.5-Chastity (%ofTotal), v0.5-Chastity (%ofValid)
DNBs with chastity values in the range of [0.5, 0.6) for the majority of cycles being analyzed.
0.6-Chastity (%ofTotal), v0.6-Chastity (%ofValid)
DNBs with chastity values in the range of [0.6, 0.7) for the majority of cycles being analyzed.
0.7-Chastity (%ofTotal), v0.7-Chastity (%ofValid)
DNBs with chastity values in the range of [0.7, 0.8) for the majority of cycles being analyzed.
0.8-Chastity (%ofTotal), v0.8-Chastity (%ofValid)
DNBs with chastity values in the range of [0.8, 0.9) for the majority of cycles being analyzed.
0.9-Chastity (%ofTotal), v0.9-Chastity (%ofValid)
DNBs with chastity values in the range of [0.9, 1.0) for the majority of cycles being analyzed.
1.0-Chastity (%ofTotal), v1.0-Chastity (%ofValid)
DNBs with chastity values above 1.0 for the majority of cycles being analyzed. (Chastity is greater than 1.0 in the case that the 2nd highest intensity is negative).
%dnbs_of_inner: Percent of DNBs excluding the 36 edge blocks
neighbors: Number of labeled DNBs that are neighbors for that label
neighbors_shuffled: Number of labeled DNBs that are neighbors for that label, after randomly shuffling
neighbors_ratio: Ratio of neighbors_shuffled to neighbors

The value in the cluster mixed summary table is the neighbors_ratio. A low ratio would indicate non-random co-localization / clustering of the labeled DNBs. A ratio close to 1.0 would indicate that the labeled DNBs are randomly distributed across the FOV. A ratio of -1 means there are 0% DNBs in the inner blocks for that label.

Labels

Empty (ofInner)
DNBs with a mean average Called Base Intensity (CBI) less than the empty threshold are declared empty. The empty threshold is calculated by measuring the distribution of noise/background in each channel of each cycle.
Multicall or Low Chastity (ofInner)
MultiCall DNBs have at least two intensities above the empty threshold (in a given cycle) for the majority of the cycles being analyzed. Chastity is calculated as the ratio of the highest intensity to the top two intensities (in a given cycle). A DNB is declared to have low chastity if it has a chastity lower than 0.7 for the majority of cycles being analyzed. This label includes DNBs with multiple call counts OR low chastity, and may include empty DNBs with low chastity.
Multicall or Low Chastity Non-Empty (ofInner)
MultiCall DNBs have at least two intensities above the empty threshold (in a given cycle) for the majority of the cycles being analyzed. A DNB is declared to have low chastity if it has a chastity lower than 0.7 for the majority of cycles being analyzed. This label includes DNBs with multiple call counts OR low chastity, excluding empty DNBs with low chastity.
Multicall and Low Chastity (ofInner)
MultiCall DNBs have at least two intensities above the empty threshold (in a given cycle) for the majority of the cycles being analyzed. A DNB is declared to have low chastity if it has a chastity lower than 0.7 for the majority of cycles being analyzed. This label includes DNBs with multiple call counts AND low chastity.
Multicall (ofInner)
MultiCall DNBs have at least two intensities above the empty threshold (in a given cycle) for the majority of the cycles being analyzed.
2-Call (ofInner)
2-Call DNBs have 2 intensities above the empty threshold (in a given cycle) for the majority of the cycles being analyzed.
3-Call (ofInner)
3-Call DNBs have 3 intensities above the empty threshold (in a given cycle) for the majority of the cycles being analyzed.
4-Call (ofInner)
4-Call DNBs have all 4 intensities above the empty threshold (in a given cycle) for the majority of the cycles being analyzed.
Low Chastity (ofInner)
A DNB is declared to have low chastity if it has a chastity lower than 0.7 for the majority of cycles being analyzed.
0.6-Chastity (ofInner)
DNBs with chastity values in the range of [0.6, 0.7) for the majority of cycles being analyzed.
0.5-Chastity (ofInner)
DNBs with chastity values in the range of [0.5, 0.6) for the majority of cycles being analyzed.
NaN-Chastity (ofInner)
DNBs with chastity values with NaN for the majority of cycles being analyzed.
Splits (%ofTotal), vSplits (%ofValid)
Splits are any DNB that matches (either by sequence or intensity pattern) any of its neighbors.
Children (%ofTotal), vChildren (%ofValid)
Splits that have lower avg CBI than all matched neighbors. They are both a Parent and a Child.
Parents (%ofTotal), vParents (%ofValid)
Splits that have higher avg CBI than all matched neighbors.
MidParent (%ofTotal), vMidParent (%ofValid)
Splits that have both a lower and higher avg CBI matched neighbor.
Horizontal Split (%ofSplits)
Proportion of splits that are from single, horizontal splits.
Vertical Split (%ofSplits)
Proportion of splits that are from single, vertical splits.
Diagonal Split (%ofSplits)
Proportion of splits that are from single, diagonal splits.
Multiple Split (%ofSplits)
Proportion of splits that are from multiple splits.
Most Frequent 10-mer
10bp sequence with the highest count of split DNBs that have that exact sequence.
Frequency of 10-mer
Counts of split DNBs with the most frequent 10bp sequence.
Left Parent Count
Count of left parents.
Right Parent Count
Count of right parents.
Up Parent Count
Count of up parents.
Down Parent Count
Count of down parents.
Empty Parents (%ofSingleParents), Empty Parents (%ofTotal)
Proportion of parents that are classified as empty.
Empty Children (%ofSingleChildren), Empty Children (%ofTotal)
Proportion of children that are classified as empty.

Proportion of splits/splits that are classified as empty.

Parents that are classified as 0-Call.
0-Call Children (%ofTotal)
Children that are classified as 0-Call.
NonSplit Mixed (%ofMixed), vNonSplit Mixed (%ofValidMixed)
Proportion of Mixed DNBs that are not Split DNBs.

Proportion of Mixed DNBs that are Children.

Proprotion of Mixed DNBs that are both a Parent and a Child.

Proportion of MultiCall DNBs that are Children.

Proprotion of MultiCall DNBs that are Parents.
mcMidParent (%ofMultiCall), vmcMidParent (%ofValidMultiCall)
Proprotion of MultiCall DNBs that are both a Parent and a Child.
lcNonSplit (%ofLowChastity), vlcNonSplit (%ofValidLowChastity)
Proportion of LowChastity DNBs that are not Split DNBs.

Total number of clusters. Adjacent DNBs that share the same sequence or intensity pattern define a cluster.

CBI: Percentile rankings for normalized average called base intensity. 1 to 100 are positive (non-empty, non-outlier) DNBs broken up into 100 subsets of equal size. Outliers are labeled 101 and empties are evenly split from -10 to -1. Nans are considered 0.
SNR1: Percentile rankings for SNR1, calculated using all cycles altogether (ReadWise)
SNR2: Percentile rankings for SNR2, calculated per cycle then averaged across all (CycleWise)
The Q1, Q2, Q3, and Q4 of CBI, SNR1, or SNR2 are reported for each of the labels.
